For fonts, use Inter as your primary choice for both headings and body text as it's clean, geometric, and excellent for tech portfolios. If you want combinations, Poppins with Open Sans gives a modern tech feel with bold headings and readable body text. Playfair Display with Lato offers elegance if you want to stand out, IBM Plex Mono with IBM Plex Sans provides technical developer vibes, and Abril Fatface with Source Sans Pro creates eye-catching headers with clean text. For your portfolio website, stick with Inter or Roboto or Sora as single fonts. For your actual resume file that goes to ATS systems, use standard fonts like Arial, Calibri, Helvetica, or Georgia only. Never use fancy fonts in the resume document itself.

When creating your ATS-friendly resume, use standard section headers like Experience, Education, Skills, and Projects instead of creative names. Keep it in a single column layout with no tables, columns, or graphics. Never include logos, images, or custom symbols. Use standard bullet points and save as a selectable PDF, not an image-based one. Your file should be named FirstName_LastName_Resume.pdf. Include relevant keywords from your tech stack throughout like React, Next.js, NestJS, TypeScript, API, REST, so the ATS system picks it up. Avoid headers and footers as ATS skips them. Format all dates as MM/YYYY or Month Year consistently.

For your skills section, list Frontend skills as React, Next.js, Tailwind CSS, TypeScript, HTML5, CSS3. Include Backend skills like NestJS, Node.js, RESTful APIs, Database Design. Add Tools and Platforms such as Git, GitHub, VS Code, Linux, and Docker if applicable. Mention AI-assisted development or Claude integration as it's a differentiator. Include soft skills like problem-solving, client communication, and project delivery.

When describing your experience at your current role and with clients like Fynnexa, Globewyze, and Revwyze, start each bullet point with impact metrics rather than generic descriptions. Highlight that you've delivered multiple client projects on schedule. Use numbers and percentages where possible to show measurable results. For your projects section in the portfolio, showcase 2-3 of your strongest projects only, not everything. Include the live link, GitHub link, and tech stack used for each. For example, your Chrome Maps Lead Scraper project should be presented as "Chrome Maps Lead Scraper, built with Next.js, Playwright, and PostgreSQL, with a GitHub link and live demo, mentioning that it automated lead generation and processed 500+ business leads." Always prioritize live demos over screenshots because they prove your work actually functions.

Design your portfolio website with minimalism and cleanliness because developers trust straightforward designs without fluff. Implement dark mode as an option because it's a current trend in developer portfolios. Ensure fast loading times by optimizing images and code-splitting. Make it mobile responsive since most visitors will be on mobile devices. Show code snippets to highlight your technical thinking. Emphasize your AI-assisted and vibe-coding development approach as a differentiator from other developers. These details matter for first impressions.

For your resume summary or about section, position yourself as a full-stack developer specializing in Next.js and NestJS who has delivered multiple client projects and uses an AI-assisted development approach. If you're concerned about market-rate compensation, subtly mention in your portfolio that you're seeking market-rate full-stack developer compensation, which helps filter recruits and sets expectations. Your education section can include your degree if it's from a strong university, or simply state "Self-taught full-stack developer" if that applies. Either works if your projects and experience are solid.

Overall structure your resume to be scannable in about 6 seconds. Your professional summary should be 1-2 sentences. Experience section should show 2-3 bullet points per role focused on impact and tech used. Skills should be organized by category. Projects section should have 2-3 entries with links. Keep everything to one page maximum. The goal is to make both the ATS system and human recruiters immediately understand that you're a capable full-stack developer with real client experience, technical depth in Next.js and NestJS, and a modern approach to development. Your portfolio website and resume should work together to tell that story consistently.
